HOLOGRAPHIC SPECTATOR

Full Video
Tools: Unity3D, Microsoft Holographics Toolkit
Languages: C#, Python
The Holographic Spectator was an experimental program built over the course of 3 days during Microsoft's company-wide hackathon by a team of four developers (including myself). The goal of the holographic spectator was to experiment with a new method of watching eSports by having the game be displayed in augmented reality. By using augmented reality, a 3d hologram of the game can be displayed on tables, countertops, or even in the middle of a room. This allows for a unique viewing experience for the viewers, where they can move around the hologram in any way that they want to view the game.
For the hackathon, we demonstrated its capabilities by creating a holographic viewing experience for Rocket League. The experience uses previously captured network data to gather information about the positions and statistics for each player, and statistics for the game, such as score and time remaining. A small field was created the display this captured data to the user. Score and time remaining are displayed on a scoredboard at the top of the field.
Since we are simply parsing data from the game, this system could be applied to nearly any game, including physical sports like baseball, football, and volleyball.
